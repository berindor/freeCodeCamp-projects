Georg Cantor

Mathematical fields Georg Cantor worked on:

  • Number Theory
  • Trigonometric Series
  • Set Theory

Most famous results:

  • One-to-one correspondence between the rational and natural number and between the real number and the points of the n-dimensional Euclidean space (1874)
  • Formulation of the Continuumhypothesis: There is no cardinality which is larger than the cardinality of the naturals and smaller than the cardinality of the real numbers (1878)
  • Well-ordering principle: Every set can be well-ordered (1883)
  • The Cantor-set is nowhere dense but have the same cardinality as the real numbers (1883)
